MEMRISE

USABILITY STUDY

What is it about?



Memrise is an online learning community where one can learn almost anything in the world. It is an online learning tool with courses created by its community. Its courses are mainly used to teach languages, but are also used for other academic and nonacademic subjects (such as trivia, video game trivia, and pop cultural). Memrise uses flashcards augmented with mnemonics and the spacing effect to boost the speed and ease of learning. We decided to evaluate this learning technology because all the team members are personal users of this website and use it occasionally to learn something new in a fun way. We saw an opportunity as we felt there are some usability issues in the website and no usability studies have been done so far. So, we want to test the usability using a bigger pool of participants and suggest improvements.



What was my role?


Created a study plan, recruited participants, set up the study using Morae, analyzed the data and created a solid design recommendation report.





The Study



    Study Type

We use summative usability study approach evaluating how well piece of functionality in Memrise meets its objective.



    Study Goal

The purpose of the study is to evaluate the user interface for new users of Memrise, an online community for learning topics and subjects, used by thousands of online learners. The research study will measure the ease with which new users understand navigability and visual hierarchy of the learning technology. We also would like to determine the overall satisfaction of the users using Memrise and eventually inform the research findings to Memrise, so that they can improve the user experience.


    Guiding Questions



  • How can we improve satisfaction level of a Memrise user?
  • How can we improve navigability of website?


      • User Research - Participants

      Participants were adults (Age greater than 18) who were interested in learning something new, were regular users of technology and had not used Memrise before. 5 participants were part of the study.



        Evaluation Method

      Descriptive Data Metrics (Demographic survey form), Performance Metrics (Morae Recorder), Post Task Rating (Morae Recorder), Self Reported Metrics (Post-session interviews and SUS)



        Data Analysis

      Usability study of Memrise website was conducted with five users. Users were asked to complete fourteen tasks as per our usability study plan. Demographic survey, Task description, SUS and Post-task survey were pre-saved in Morae configuration file. The researchers then took a qualitative interview of all participants. The data was captured using Morae software and was further analyzed using Morae Manager and Microsoft Excel to provide constructive feedback and recommendation on the usability of the Memrise website.
